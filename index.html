Kathmandu Living Labs: Six-Months of Earthquake Response

National Housing Reconstruction Program

KLL signs the contract with UNOPS to assist Nepal Government collect the data for National Housing Reconstruction Program. KLL developed an app called HRD Collect which is used by 1600 engineers in 14 earthquake affected districts. They are collecting building damage data, socio-economic data and demographic data.

More October 2015

Cultural Heritage Damage Assessment

KLL in collaboration with UNESCO and support of DoA mobilized volunteers to collect initial damage assessment data of cultural heritages in and around Kathmandu Valley. A publicly accessible site is created and handed over to DoA.


November 10, 2015

School Infrastructural Damage Assessment

The Department of Education and The World Bank ask KLL to support an assessment of damage to schools because of KLL’s expertise in mobile data collection technology. KLL developed a mobile data collection app KLL Collect SIDA. 2400 Civil engineers were deployed in the field in phase I of the project ((Districts: Ramechhap, Kavrepalanchowk, Kathmandu, Lalitpur, Bhaktapur, Okhaldhunga, Makwanpur, Sindhuli). Phase I is complete and the phase II (Districts: Nuwakot, Rasuwa, Gorkha, Dhading, Sindhupalchok and Dolakha) started from Dec 17, 2015.

November 16, 2015

Global Shapers Event

Global Shapers Kathmandu hub approached KLL to arrange a mapping event for their Global Shapers South Asia guests. Global Shapers South Asia was organized in Nepal for the first time and KLL was among the four hosts. KLL hosted 15 global shapers from 8 cities around South Asia and shared different experiences of the works post-earthquake. There was also a mapping event organized for them.

November 21, 2015

Epinurse

KLL signed a contract with Epinurse, a Japanese Government funded program that lets Japanese researchers collaborate with foreign nationals. KLL developed a mobile collection app which could capture different health parameters of IDP camps. The app is capable of collecting data offline and the data was available to the central staff immediately on submission.

Secondary Cities

Pokhara was selected among the four cities for the Secondary Cities project and KLL, from Nepal, will lead this project. KLL will reflect and apply the learnings of the post-earthquake work and prepare Pokhara are any kind of disaster in the field of data and information. A three day national level workshop “Learning from the April 25 Earthquake to Prepare for Future Disasters: Creating an Open Map Infrastructure in Pokhara” brought local people from diverse background into a common platform where they shared their knowledge, experiences and built up a network as a foundation of future works together.

Feb 28, 2016

Open Data Day

KLL co-organised Open Data Day in Pokhara in collaboration with GESAN, student club at Western Regional College and Research Management Unit, Tribhuwan University. There were talk sessions (Dr. Nama Raj Budhathoki, Dr. Swarnim Wagle and Hon. Mr. Rabindra Adhikari) followed by mapathon, data expedition and geocaching running in parallel. This one day event hosted around 300 participants. This was the first time Open Data Day was celebrated in Pokhara.
